# ff-positioning

Position your product to stand out in crowded markets. Covers niche selection, value propositions (JTBD), competitive differentiation (Blue Ocean, Porter's), positioning frameworks (April Dunford), and messaging.

Built for founders building in competitive spaces who need strategic depth, not just "be unique" advice.

## Skills (6)

- **find-niche** - Find your niche when you can build anything but don't know who for. Uses Porter's generic strategies, Blue Ocean thinking, and market demand signals to identify niches with real opportunity.
- **positioning-canvas** - Position your product against alternatives using April Dunford's 5-component framework from "Obviously Awesome." Defines competitive alternatives, unique attributes, value, target customer, and market category.
- **value-proposition** - Build a 6-part value proposition using the JTBD (Jobs to Be Done) framework. Covers Who, Why, What Before, How, What After, and Alternatives. Includes value curve mapping.
- **differentiation-audit** - Find what makes your product different across 8 dimensions. Uses Blue Ocean Strategy's four actions framework and competitive value curve analysis.
- **elevator-pitch** - Craft a clear pitch in three lengths: one-liner, problem-solution, and 60-second story. Plus context-specific variations for landing pages, social bios, outreach, and communities.
- **product-name** - Brainstorm product names using 5 naming strategies. Evaluates on memorability, pronounceability, relevance, distinctiveness, and availability.

## Commands (1)

- `/ff-positioning:position` - Run a full positioning workflow: niche -> value proposition -> differentiation audit -> positioning canvas -> pitch. Produces a comprehensive positioning strategy document.

## Who This Is For

- You're in a crowded space and everything looks the same
- You can't articulate why someone should choose your product over alternatives
- You know your product is good but can't explain it clearly
- You need strategic positioning, not just a tagline generator
