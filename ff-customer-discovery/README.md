# ff-customer-discovery

Understand who your app is actually for. Target audience identification, customer interview prep, competitor scanning, persona building, and demand signal reading - all built for solo founders who shipped first and asked questions later.

Built for vibecoders - people who build apps with AI tools (Cursor, Replit, Bolt, Lovable) but skip the "who is this even for?" step.

## Skills (6)

- **find-target-audience** - Identify who your app is REALLY for, not who you assumed. Scores audience segments on pain intensity, willingness to pay, reachability, market size, and founder-market fit to find your best beachhead.
- **customer-interview-script** - Create a Mom Test-style interview script that surfaces real insights instead of compliments. Includes probing techniques, note-taking template, and inline reminders to stop you from pitching.
- **summarize-interview** - Extract actionable insights from interview transcripts. Flags Mom Test violations, identifies JTBD, and pulls the one insight that should change what you build.
- **competitor-scan** - Lightweight competitor analysis for solo founders. Maps 5-7 competitors, applies simplified Porter's Five Forces, builds a value curve, and identifies the gap where you win.
- **user-personas** - Create 2-3 data-driven personas from real research. JTBD-based, not demographic fiction. Clearly marks unvalidated hypothesis personas when data is missing.
- **demand-signals** - Read demand signals from Reddit, Twitter/X, forums, and search behaviour. Checks 5 signal sources and scores overall demand confidence before you invest more time.

## Commands (1)

- `/ff-customer-discovery:discover-customers` - Run a full customer discovery workflow: find your target audience, scan competitors, validate demand, prepare interview scripts, and build hypothesis personas. Produces a complete Customer Discovery Report.

## Who This Is For

- You shipped an app and got crickets - now you need to figure out who actually wants it
- You have users but don't understand why they signed up or what they really need
- You're about to build something and want to find your audience before writing code
- You've never done a customer interview and don't know where to start
- You want to know if real demand exists for what you're building
