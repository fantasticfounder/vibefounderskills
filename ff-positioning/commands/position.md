# /position -- Full Positioning Strategy

Position your product to stand out: find your niche, define your value proposition, audit your differentiation, and craft your pitch - all in one workflow.

## Invocation

```
/position AI meeting notes tool for freelancers
/position Shopify analytics dashboard
/position [paste your product description]
```

## Workflow

### Step 1: Understand the Product and Market

Ask:
- What does your product do? (one sentence)
- Who is it currently for? (or who do you think it's for?)
- What's the competitive landscape? Who else does something similar?
- What do YOU think makes your product different?
- Have you done any customer research or validation?

Accept context from uploaded files, competitor research, or conversation.

If the founder's product description is less than 3 sentences, ask clarifying questions before proceeding. If a full paragraph or more, proceed and surface gaps during checkpoints.

### Step 2: Find or Confirm Your Niche

Apply the **find-niche** skill:

- Map founder assets (skills, domain knowledge, network)
- Generate 5-8 niche candidates
- Score each on demand, competition, willingness to pay, founder-market fit, reachability, and growth potential
- Validate top 2-3 with quick search and community checks
- Recommend one niche with rationale

**Checkpoint**: "Based on the analysis, [niche] is your strongest positioning opportunity. Here's why. Does this feel right?"

If the user has already validated their niche (through prior work or validate-idea), acknowledge it and move to Step 3.

### Step 3: Build the Value Proposition

Apply the **value-proposition** skill:

- Define all 6 JTBD components: Who, Why, What Before, How, What After, Alternatives
- Write the value proposition statement
- Skip the value curve in this step - it will be produced in Step 4 (differentiation-audit) instead. This avoids duplicate output.

**Checkpoint**: "Here's your value proposition. Does this accurately describe your product's value? Is anything missing or wrong?"

### Step 4: Audit Differentiation

Apply the **differentiation-audit** skill:

- Map the competitive landscape (direct, indirect, manual, do nothing)
- Analyse differentiation across 8 dimensions (product, audience, experience, price, distribution, content, support, story)
- Apply Blue Ocean Strategy four actions framework (eliminate, reduce, raise, create)
- Create a value curve
- Identify the decisive differentiator

**Checkpoint**: "Here's your differentiation audit. Your decisive differentiator is [X]. Does this match your intuition? Is there a differentiator we're missing?"

### Step 5: Build the Positioning Canvas

Apply the **positioning-canvas** skill:

- Define all 5 components (competitive alternatives, unique attributes, value, target customer, market category)
- Write the positioning statement
- Test against 5 positioning checks (clarity, specificity, truthfulness, differentiation, relevance)

**Checkpoint**: "Here's your positioning statement. Does it feel true and specific? Would your ideal customer immediately recognise themselves in it?"

### Step 6: Craft the Pitch

Apply the **elevator-pitch** skill:

- Write three pitch lengths: one-liner, problem-solution, story pitch
- Create context variations (Twitter bio, landing page headline, Reddit intro, cold outreach opener)
- Test with So What?, Repetition, and Who's It For? checks

### Step 7: Generate the Full Positioning Report

Note: Full skill outputs from each step should be saved as separate files (e.g., `[slug]-find-niche.md`, `[slug]-value-proposition.md`). This report is a summary that ties everything together.

## Notes

- Allow 90-120 minutes across multiple exchanges
- At each checkpoint, the user can redirect or go deeper
- Positioning should be revisited every 6 months or when the market changes significantly
- The strongest positioning comes from real customer conversations, not just analysis. If the user hasn't talked to customers yet, flag this.
