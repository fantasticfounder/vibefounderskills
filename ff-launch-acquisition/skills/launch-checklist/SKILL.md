# Launch Checklist

A structured launch plan across three phases: pre-launch preparation, launch day execution, and post-launch momentum. Based on proven launch frameworks adapted for solo founders and small teams.

## Context

You are helping a founder plan their product launch for: **$ARGUMENTS**.

If the user provides details about their product, audience, channels, or timeline, read those first.

## The Launch Framework

Most launches fail not because the product is bad, but because the founder treated launch day as a single event instead of a campaign. A launch is three phases:

1. **Pre-launch (2-4 weeks before)**: Build anticipation, prepare assets, line up distribution
2. **Launch day (1-2 days)**: Coordinate across channels, maximise visibility
3. **Post-launch (2-4 weeks after)**: Sustain momentum, convert attention into users, gather feedback

### Step 1: Assess Launch Readiness

Before planning the launch, verify the foundations:

| Readiness Check | Status |
|---|---|
| Product works and is accessible (can someone sign up right now?) | |
| Landing page clearly explains what the product does | |
| One-sentence pitch is crisp and tested | |
| Pricing is set (even if "free for now") | |
| Analytics are tracking sign-ups and sources | |
| Beachhead segment is defined (who you're launching TO) | |
| 2-3 channels are identified (from channel-scorer) | |

If 1-2 items are missing, note them at the top of the plan and add pre-launch tasks to address them. If 3+ items are missing, recommend completing these before planning the launch. Offer to help with each missing item. A premature launch wastes your "first impression" across every channel.

### Step 2: Pre-Launch Phase (2-4 Weeks Before)

**Week -4 to -3: Build the Foundation**

- Write your launch narrative - not a feature list, but a STORY. Why does this product exist? What problem did you hit personally? What makes this different?
- Prepare visual assets: product screenshots, demo GIF or video (30-60 seconds), before/after comparison if applicable
- Create a waitlist or early access page if the product isn't fully ready
- Draft all launch copy: landing page, email announcement, social posts, community posts

**Week -2 to -1: Build Anticipation**

- Start sharing the journey on your primary channel (build-in-public posts, teasers, behind-the-scenes)
- Engage in communities where your beachhead segment hangs out - add value first, don't pitch yet
- Line up supporters: DM 10-20 people who might genuinely care and ask if they'd be willing to check it out on launch day
- If using ProductHunt: recruit 5+ hunters, prepare all PH assets, choose your launch day (Tuesday-Thursday typically best)
- If using Reddit: identify 3-5 specific subreddits with clear posting rules, draft posts tailored to each community's culture
- If using cold outreach: prepare personalised email templates, build a list of 30-50 prospects

**Week -1: Final Prep**

- Test the full user journey: landing page -> sign up -> onboarding -> core value delivery
- Prepare a launch day schedule (hour by hour if needed)
- Pre-write all posts and emails - don't write under pressure on launch day
- Set up monitoring: how will you track real-time sign-ups, social mentions, community responses?

### Step 3: Launch Day Execution

**Coordination is everything.** Launch across channels within the same 24-48 hour window to create the feeling of momentum.

Adapt this schedule to your primary channel. If your primary channel is cold outreach, there is no 'launch hour' event - spread outreach over Week 1 instead of concentrating it on one day.

**Hour-by-hour template:**

| Time | Action |
|---|---|
| Morning (before launch) | Final check: landing page, sign-up flow, analytics |
| Launch hour | Publish on primary channel (PH, Reddit, HN, or wherever) |
| +1 hour | Post on secondary channels (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, IndieHackers) |
| +2 hours | Send email to waitlist / early access list |
| +3-4 hours | Respond to EVERY comment, question, and mention |
| Afternoon | Share on remaining channels (directories, communities, groups) |
| Evening | Check metrics, respond to new comments, engage with anyone who shared |
| End of day | Post a "thank you" update with early numbers if they're good |

**Launch day rules:**
- Respond to every single comment. Engagement signals to algorithms and people that you're present and care.
- Don't argue with critics. Thank them for feedback. Fix real issues fast.
- Be transparent about what the product does and doesn't do. Overpromising on launch day creates churn.
- Have a friend or supporter ready to answer questions if you get overwhelmed.

### Step 4: Post-Launch Phase (2-4 Weeks After)

Launch day gives you attention. Post-launch converts attention into retained users.

**Week +1: Capitalise on Momentum**
- Follow up with everyone who signed up - personal welcome email or message
- Publish a "lessons learned" or "launch results" post (founders love reading these, drives more traffic)
- Fix the top 3 bugs or friction points users reported
- Ask your happiest early users for testimonials or reviews

**Week +2: Content and SEO**
- Write a 'We launched and here's what happened' post with real metrics for IndieHackers/Reddit. Template: [X sign-ups on day 1], [what went wrong], [what surprised us], [what we're doing next]. This reliably drives second-wave traffic.
- Cross-post the launch story to channels you missed
- Submit to directories and listing sites you haven't covered yet
- Start your email list if you haven't already

**Week +3-4: Retention and Feedback Loop**
- Check activation rates: of people who signed up, how many actually used the product?
- If activation is low, the problem is onboarding, not acquisition
- Conduct 5-10 short user interviews (what worked, what's confusing, what's missing)
- Identify your best acquisition channel and increase effort there

### Step 5: Handle Launch Platform Realities

Be strategic about where you launch and what to expect:

**ProductHunt**
- Pros: High visibility in the tech/startup ecosystem, social proof, backlinks
- Cons: Audience is largely other founders and tech enthusiasts, not necessarily your customers. Launch day traffic often doesn't convert to retained users. The "upvote culture" means you're competing for attention with dozens of launches daily.
- Best for: Developer tools, design tools, productivity apps where the PH audience overlaps with your target segment
- Strategy: Treat PH as a PR event, not an acquisition channel. The badge and backlinks have more long-term value than the launch day traffic.

**Reddit**
- Pros: Authentic engagement, niche communities, high-intent users if you find the right subreddit
- Cons: Anti-self-promotion culture, posts can get removed, volatile (great post can die, mediocre post can blow up)
- Strategy: Lead with value and story, not features. "I built this because I had this problem" > "Check out my new app!"

**Hacker News (Show HN)**
- Pros: Technical audience, high-quality feedback, can drive significant traffic
- Cons: Extremely unpredictable, harsh criticism is common, traffic spike is short-lived
- Strategy: Post early in the day (US time), focus on the technical or novel aspect, be ready for brutal feedback

## Notes

- You only get one "launch" per platform. Don't waste it by launching before you're ready.
- Launch is the beginning of marketing, not the end. The real work starts after launch day.
- If you're unsure about timing, launch sooner rather than later. A "perfect" launch in 3 months is worse than a "good enough" launch this week.
- The best launches tell a story people want to share, not a feature list people scroll past.
